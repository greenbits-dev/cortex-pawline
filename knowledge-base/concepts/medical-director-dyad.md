# Medical Director dyad (RMD + ROD)

Pawline's regional leadership unit. Every region is led by a paired **Regional Medical Director (DVM)** and **Regional Operations Director (non-clinical)**. Both report up — the RMD to [[dr-elena-vasquez]] (CMO), the ROD to [[marcus-kim]] (COO) — and to each other laterally.

## Scope of authority

| Decision | RMD | ROD |
|----------|-----|-----|
| Clinical protocol exceptions | Sole | Inform |
| Hiring DVMs | Approve | Inform |
| Hiring practice managers | Inform | Approve |
| Capex >$25K | Joint | Joint |
| Quality audits | Sole | Inform |
| Vendor escalations | Inform | Sole |
| Comp exceptions for DVMs | Joint | Joint |
| Real-estate / facilities | Inform | Sole |
| New clinic acquisitions (regional fit veto) | Joint | Joint |

The dyad is the integration unit: any region-level decision needing both clinical and operational input flows through the pair, not through a single boss.

## Why this works

[[nps-correlates-with-medical-director-engagement]] showed that clinics whose RMD did monthly chart rounds posted +18 NPS points. The dyad model formalizes the time it takes — the RMD doesn't have to also chase facilities issues and lease renewals. Clinical leadership stays clinical.

## History

- Q3 2024: piloted in West region with [[dr-rachel-chen]] and a now-departed ROD
- Q1 2025: adopted as standard
- Q4 2025: [[2025-11-10-medical-director-dyad-rollout]] — formal rollout decision across all regions
- Today: West has open ROD seat; Mountain and Southwest fully staffed

## Risks

- High-quality ROD candidates are scarce. The model only works if both sides of the dyad are strong.
- "Solid line" vs "dotted line" reporting requires clear cadence to avoid drift; the weekly ops review and monthly clinical council enforce it.
