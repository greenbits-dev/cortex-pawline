# Hub-and-spoke clinic model

Pawline's regional operating model. Each region is anchored by one or more flagship (Tier A) clinics that provide surgical suites, ER/specialty referral capacity, and overflow capability for surrounding satellite (Tier B/C) clinics. Satellites refer up; flagships do not refer out except for true tertiary care.

## How it works

- **Hub (Tier A flagship)** — full surgical suite, dental specialty equipment, in-house cardiology or internal-medicine specialist 2-5 days/week, 24-hour staffing on the ER footprint where present
- **Spoke (Tier B GP)** — full general practice, basic dentistry, basic surgery (spays/neuters), routine wellness — refers complex cases to the hub
- **Spoke (Tier C satellite)** — limited-service, often acquired smaller clinics not yet upgraded, focused on wellness and minor sick-visit care

## Why this model

- **Capital efficiency**: surgical suites cost $400-800K to build out. Spreading one suite across 6-12 clinics is the difference between 35% margins and 12% margins on the cohort.
- **Doctor career path**: Tier C → B → A is a reasonable career progression for a DVM. Aligns with [[clinical-career-ladder]].
- **Client retention**: keeping surgical and specialty work inside the Pawline network instead of referring out to independent specialty hospitals captures revenue and continuity.

## Where it strains

- Acquired clinics that were stand-alone full-service practices sometimes resent "demotion" to Tier B. Integration playbook ([[acquisition-integration-playbook]]) addresses this with explicit messaging.
- In rural CO and UT, density doesn't always support a true hub — some clusters operate as "loosely federated peers" without a clear flagship.
