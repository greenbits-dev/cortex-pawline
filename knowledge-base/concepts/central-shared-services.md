# Central shared services

What Pawline runs centrally vs. what stays local at the clinic / region. Pawline's bias is to centralize anything that delivers economies of scale without compromising clinical autonomy.

## Centralized

- **Procurement** (surgical supplies, drugs, consumables) — see [[2025-04-15-centralize-surgical-procurement]]
- **Lab vendor relationships** — Antech-primary, Idexx-secondary; see [[lab-vendor-consolidation-savings]]
- **PIMS / IT infrastructure** — Cornerstone licensing, networks, security; owned by [[priya-shah]]
- **Finance** — GL, AP, AR, payroll, treasury (clinic-level QuickBooks retired post-acquisition)
- **HR** — talent acquisition for DVMs, comp/benefits administration, equity program
- **M&A** — pipeline, diligence, integration
- **Marketing** — brand standards, digital advertising, Salesforce Service Cloud
- **Compliance** — DEA, OSHA, state veterinary practice acts, insurance / liability

## Local (clinic / region)

- **Clinical decisions** — within the [[standard-of-care-protocols]] envelope, the DVM has discretion
- **Practice manager hiring** — region owns
- **Technician / staff hiring** — clinic owns
- **Day-to-day scheduling** — clinic owns
- **Pricing within published ranges** — clinic owns local price levels within [[comp-framework-2026]] guidance (different document for clinical pricing)
- **Community / charity engagement** — clinic owns

## Why this split

The centralization wave between 2019-2024 was opportunistic and uneven. The 2025 governance pass formalized the split with one principle: **scale concerns centralize, judgment concerns localize**. Anything where uniformity beats local autonomy moves up; anything where local context drives quality stays down.

## Tension points

- Acquired clinic owners often resent procurement centralization in the first 90 days. The [[acquisition-integration-playbook]] addresses this.
- Marketing centralization drove pushback in CO + UT (cohort sensitive to "corporate feel"). The [[Pawline Endorsement]] co-branding model is partly a response.
