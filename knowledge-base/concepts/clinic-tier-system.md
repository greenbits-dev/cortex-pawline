# Clinic tier system (A/B/C)

Pawline's three-level clinic classification. Tier determines capability, capex envelope, staffing model, and the kind of cases the clinic accepts.

## The tiers

| Tier | What it does | Typical revenue | DVMs | Surgical | Specialty |
|------|--------------|-----------------|------|----------|-----------|
| **A — Flagship** | Full GP + surgery + specialty referral hub | $4-8M/yr | 5-10 | Full surgical suite | 1-2 specialists 2-5 days/wk |
| **B — Full GP** | Full general practice, routine surgery | $2.5-4M/yr | 2-4 | Spay/neuter only | None |
| **C — Satellite** | Wellness + light sick-visit | $1-2.5M/yr | 1-2 | None | None |

## How tier is assigned

- New acquisitions: assigned at close based on surgical equipment, square footage, doctor count, and historical revenue
- Tier changes: require both RMD and ROD sign-off, COO approval if upgrading to Tier A (capex implications)
- Most acquired clinics enter as Tier B; ~15% enter as Tier C; flagship Tier A clinics are typically built or acquired explicitly to be flagships

## Capex implications

Tier A clinics get the surgical suite, dental ortho equipment, and digital radiography upgrades. Tier B clinics get the wellness/dental upgrade path. Tier C clinics get the minimum-viable maintenance budget. The [[2025-04-15-centralize-surgical-procurement]] decision keys procurement off tier.

## Tension with acquisitions

Sellers often want their clinic classified as Tier A. The honest classification is what determines integration debt down the line — see [[acquired-clinic-revenue-dip-month-3]]. We classify on the data, not on seller expectations.
