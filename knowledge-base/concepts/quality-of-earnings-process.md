# Quality of Earnings (QofE) process

Pawline's pre-LOI financial diligence. The work that determines whether a target's reported EBITDA is real, what's recurring, and what's noise. Owned jointly by [[dr-james-okwu]] (corporate dev) and [[tom-brennan]] (CFO).

## The process

### Stage 1 — Pre-LOI scrub (1-2 weeks)

- **Financial statements** — TTM P&L by month, balance sheet, working-capital schedule
- **Tax returns** — past 3 years, reconciled to financials
- **Revenue cuts** — by service line, by DVM, by client cohort, by month for seasonality
- **Add-back review** — every add-back tested against [[ebitda-add-backs-policy]]
- **Cost normalization** — owner comp normalized to market, family members on payroll identified, personal expenses isolated
- **Working capital** — AR aging, AP aging, inventory turnover

Output: a QofE memo with a normalized EBITDA range and a confidence band.

### Stage 2 — LOI

LOI references the normalized EBITDA range. Multiple is applied to the *defended* EBITDA, not the seller-asserted EBITDA.

### Stage 3 — Confirmatory diligence (4-6 weeks)

- **Third-party QofE** for deals >$10M
- **Operational diligence** — chart audits ([[clinical-quality-review]] standards), payroll integrity, vendor contracts, lease review
- **Legal diligence** — entity, IP, litigation, employment agreements
- **Real estate** — lease vs. own, fair-market rent if owned by seller-related entity

### Stage 4 — Definitive agreement + close

- Working capital target agreed and trued up at close
- Earnout milestones tied to defended numbers, not stretch numbers

## What we look for

- **Recurring vs. one-time**: any one-time revenue spike (pet-insurance reimbursements, COVID-era stimulus, PPP-related noise) is excluded
- **Owner-perks**: cars, health insurance excess, continuing-education allowances above market — typically added back
- **Family payroll**: spouse-on-payroll or child-on-payroll requires market-rate testing
- **Pet-discount programs**: heavy owner-staff discounting on personal pets is normalized
- **AR write-offs**: above-industry write-offs raise a flag

## Where deals die in QofE

- Owner-comp normalization eats the deal multiple
- AR is materially uncollectible, eroding working capital
- Revenue concentration in a small number of corporate clients (e.g., a kennel contract that's 30% of revenue)
- Add-backs that don't survive defensible review
