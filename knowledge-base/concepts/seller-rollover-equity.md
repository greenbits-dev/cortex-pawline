# Seller rollover equity

The portion of acquisition proceeds a seller takes as equity in Pawline rather than cash. A standard PE-rollup tool, used by Pawline to align selling-DVM incentives with platform growth in the years after sale.

## When we offer it

- Seller is staying as a clinical-leadership figure (Medical Director, Regional Medical Director track)
- Deal is in the $4M+ range (below that, the rollover percentage is too small to matter)
- Seller is willing to remain involved 24+ months
- Cluster fit is good enough that the seller is genuinely betting on the regional outcome, not just on Pawline overall

## Typical structure

- **Cash at close**: 70-85% of consideration
- **Rollover equity**: 10-20% of consideration, in Pawline restricted units
- **Earnout**: 0-15% based on agreed performance milestones (rare but available)

## Vesting

Per [[equity-vesting-policy]]:
- 4-year cliff/quarterly vest standard
- Acceleration on Pawline change of control (sale, IPO)
- Forfeiture on for-cause termination
- Pro-rata vesting on death/disability

## Valuation reference

- Pawline's last 409A is the floor for granting rollover equity
- Seller can request higher reference if they believe the platform value is higher; Pawline rarely accepts this — the 409A is the disciplined number
- Larkspur's preferred-stock waterfall sits above rollover equity in the cap table

## Why this works for Pawline

- **Seller alignment**: a selling DVM with rollover equity is more invested in the post-close clinical and operational outcome
- **Cash conservation**: in a mid-market roll-up, total cash deployed matters; the rollover reduces drag on the credit facility
- **Cultural integration**: rollover sellers tend to be more receptive to brand/protocol integration than pure-cash sellers

## Why this can fail

- A seller who took rollover and exits the clinical role early (for personal or health reasons) ends up holding equity without engagement — the alignment evaporates
- If Pawline exit timing slips, the rollover holder may resent the long path to liquidity

## Take-up rate

- ~55% of eligible deals over $4M include a rollover component
- Average rollover percentage on accepting deals: 13%
