# Acquisition criteria

The published filter Pawline applies to potential acquisitions. Before a target gets diligenced, it has to clear these gates. Targets that don't fit are either passed entirely or held in long-term watch lists, not pushed through diligence.

## The criteria

### Geographic fit

- **Cluster-fit**: target sits within 90 minutes of an existing Pawline flagship, OR enables a new cluster of 4+ clinics in a target market
- **State**: CA, AZ, NV, CO, UT, TX (existing footprint). Adjacent states (OR, WA, NM) considered case-by-case if cluster economics work.
- **Density**: a single rural target with no nearby Pawline presence is almost always a pass

### Size

- **Revenue**: $2.0-12M annual (single clinic) or $8-50M (small chain)
- Below $2M: doesn't justify integration cost
- Above $50M: Pawline's bid posture isn't competitive against the larger consolidators

### Doctor base

- **DVM count**: 2+ DVMs (single-doctor practices are higher-risk integration)
- **Medical director**: existing strong MD increases the score; absent MD is workable in a cluster context
- **Tenure**: average DVM tenure >18 months at the practice

### Financial

- **EBITDA threshold**: 12% adjusted EBITDA minimum at the small end ($2-4M revenue), 15% at the larger end. Lower can work if it's a cluster builder.
- **Add-backs**: must be defensible per [[ebitda-add-backs-policy]]; target sellers who insist on aggressive add-backs are a yellow flag
- **Multiple**: target multiple range 5.5-9x adjusted EBITDA, paid mix of cash + earnout + rollover equity

### Cultural fit

- **Clinical quality** evidenced by chart audit during diligence (must clear the [[clinical-quality-review]] floor)
- **Owner-DVM willing to stay** for at least 24 months post-close (with negotiated terms)
- **No material claims history** (malpractice, employment, regulatory)

## Where the criteria are bending right now

The [[2026-01-22-q3-ma-pause]] is partly because the available Q2-Q3 pipeline includes targets that meet financial criteria but fail cultural-fit or cluster-fit gates. Saying no requires discipline; not saying no creates the integration debt.
