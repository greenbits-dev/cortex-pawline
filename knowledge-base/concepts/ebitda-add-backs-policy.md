# EBITDA add-backs policy

Pawline's published list of acceptable and unacceptable EBITDA add-backs in QofE. Disciplined add-back acceptance is the difference between paying 7x for $4M of "real" EBITDA and paying 7x for $5M of "creative" EBITDA — a $7M gap that rarely shows up post-close in cash terms.

## Acceptable add-backs

- **Owner compensation normalized to market** — Pawline-equivalent comp for the role; the difference is added back
- **One-time legal/accounting fees** — clearly transactional, not operating
- **Discontinued service lines** — revenue + cost of clearly retired offerings
- **Family payroll above market** — the above-market portion only
- **Owner-paid personal expenses** routed through the business — fitness memberships, personal travel, personal vehicle expenses
- **Above-market related-party rent** — the difference between paid rent and fair-market rent (if Pawline is taking over the lease at FMR)
- **One-time relocation or build-out costs** — capex masquerading as opex
- **One-time technology migration costs** that are clearly non-recurring

## Unacceptable add-backs

- **Owner "salary inefficiency"** — i.e., owner working too few hours and adding back projected hours of additional production. No.
- **Marketing scale-up** — "we'd spend $200K more on marketing if we were Pawline-sized." That's a buyer adjustment, not a seller add-back.
- **Future technology savings** — Cornerstone migration savings, dbt model efficiency, etc. These are buyer-side synergies and stay buyer-side.
- **Regulatory expense reversals** — DEA fines, OSHA penalties, malpractice settlements
- **Customer concentration adjustments** — losing a single account is operating risk, not an add-back
- **Personal pet care costs** routed through the business as "discount programs" without documentation
- **Bonuses paid to staff that "won't continue"** — only credible if there's a documented plan to discontinue, otherwise these are recurring

## Default position

If an add-back can't be cleanly explained in 90 seconds, it doesn't get added back. The CFO ([[tom-brennan]]) is the final word; the Head of M&A ([[dr-james-okwu]]) defers on add-back disputes.

## Why we publish this

Sellers who go into negotiations expecting Pawline to accept aggressive add-backs disqualify themselves quickly. Publishing the policy upstream filters the pipeline before diligence cost is incurred.
