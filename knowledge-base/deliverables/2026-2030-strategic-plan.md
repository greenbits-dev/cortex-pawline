# 2026-2030 Strategic Plan

The 5-year plan: where Pawline aims to be at the Larkspur exit horizon (~2030). Approved at the December 2025 board. Note: distribution is restricted to exec + board + RMDs (regional leadership-tier readers).

## Targets at horizon (2030)

- **Clinic count**: 130-150
- **Revenue**: $440-510M
- **EBITDA margin**: 19-21% blended
- **Geographic footprint**: existing 6 states + 2 new (likely OR + WA, opportunistically NM)
- **Service-line mix**: ~80% GP, ~12% UC, ~8% ER/specialty (vs current 85/10/5)

## Strategic priorities (in order)

1. **Quality at scale** — protect clinical quality through every acquisition; this is the cultural moat ([[medical-director-dyad]], [[clinical-quality-review]], [[standard-of-care-protocols]])
2. **Cluster density** — favor cluster infill over geographic expansion; finish California, finish Arizona, finish Colorado before adding states ([[regional-cluster-economics]])
3. **Service-line breadth** — formalize the ER/specialty line either organically or via acquisition ([[2026-03-30-emergency-clinic-line-of-service]])
4. **Telehealth scale** — extend telehealth from 6-clinic pilot to network-wide if Q3 2026 evaluation supports
5. **Operational excellence** — close the legacy-PIMS migration debt by 2027 ([[pims-migration-pathway]]); centralize remaining shared-services categories

## Capital deployment plan

- **Acquisitions**: $300-400M cumulative deployment 2026-2030, varying by year ([[2026-01-22-q3-ma-pause]] is one slow year)
- **Capex (organic)**: $80-120M cumulative for facility upgrades, surgical-suite expansion, and IT
- **ER/specialty line**: TBD pending [[2026-03-30-emergency-clinic-line-of-service]] resolution

## Exit thesis

Larkspur's preferred exit pathway at horizon:
1. Strategic acquisition by a larger consolidator (Mars, NVA, others) — historical comp multiples 11-13x EBITDA
2. PE secondary to a larger fund — 8-10x EBITDA
3. IPO — possible at scale but Pawline alone is sub-IPO-size

## Risks

- Multiple compression in vet-services M&A market
- Talent shortage (DVMs, especially specialists) constraining growth
- Large competitor consolidation (Mars, etc.) bidding up acquisition targets ([[competitor-acquisition-multiples-rising]])
- Macroeconomic recession affecting consumer pet spend (less elastic than other categories but not recession-proof)
