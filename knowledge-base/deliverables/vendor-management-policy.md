# Vendor Management Policy

Pawline's policy on how vendors are selected, contracted, reviewed, and renewed. Audience: anyone authorized to make purchases or sign vendor agreements. Owner: COO + Head of Procurement (rolled into COO function). See [[vendor-selection-playbook]] for the operational procedure.

## Categories

- **Strategic vendors** — top spend, long contracts, exec-team-managed (Idexx, Antech, Salesforce, Snowflake, ADP, NetSuite)
- **Approved vendors** — central catalog, no clinic-level negotiation
- **Local vendors** — clinic-level discretion within approved categories (local food/coffee for client-services, local linen services, etc.)

## What requires central approval

- Anything over $25K annual spend
- New vendor contracts in clinical-supply, IT, or HR categories
- Any contract over 24 months
- Any vendor exception to the central catalog (e.g., a clinic-specific surgical supplier)

## Selection criteria

Per [[vendor-selection-playbook]]:
1. Clinical/operational fit
2. Pricing
3. Service quality + responsiveness
4. Financial stability (top categories only)
5. Cultural fit for long-term relationships

## Contract management

- All contracts logged in NetSuite contract module
- Renewals flagged 6 months pre-expiration
- Renewals over $50K annual go through a procurement review

## Performance review

- Strategic vendors: annual relationship review
- Approved vendors: quarterly performance dashboard
- Local vendors: clinic-level discretion

## Termination / dispute

- Standard 60-90 day notice for non-strategic
- Strategic vendors: per contract, generally 6+ months notice
- Disputes escalate to General Counsel (currently outside fractional counsel)

## Why this exists

Pre-2024, every clinic managed its own vendor relationships, creating the situation that drove [[lab-vendor-consolidation-savings]] and [[2025-04-15-centralize-surgical-procurement]]. The policy preserves clinic flexibility for genuinely local categories while centralizing where scale matters.
