# Brand Book v3

Pawline's brand identity guide. Voice, visual identity, signage standards, the Pawline Endorsement co-branding model. Owner: VP of Marketing (Director-level role, reports to COO). Distributed to every clinic and every external creative partner.

## What's in the book

- **The Pawline story** — founder origin, mission, values
- **Voice & tone** — warm, vet-first, never corporate, never sentimental
- **Visual identity** — logo (master mark + endorsement variant), color palette, typography, photography style
- **Signage standards** — exterior signage at acquired clinics, interior wayfinding, lobby treatment
- **Pawline Endorsement** — co-branding model for acquired clinics retaining legacy local names
- **Digital standards** — website templates, social media templates, email templates
- **Print standards** — business cards, letterhead, client-facing print materials
- **Vehicle wraps** — for clinics with mobile units (~12 clinics have mobile/satellite vehicles)

## The Pawline Endorsement

For acquired clinics with strong local goodwill in their legacy name, the endorsement model retains the legacy name with "A Pawline Practice" added in a tertiary visual position. Used by ~40% of acquired clinics. The decision is made during integration with the regional dyad and the clinic owner; defaults to endorsement model unless local conditions argue for full Pawline branding.

## What changed in v3

- Updated photography guidelines (more diverse representation, less stock-photo aesthetic)
- Tightened the Pawline Endorsement variants (was 5, now 3)
- Added vehicle wrap standards (omitted in v2)
- Added social-media short-video standards (TikTok / Instagram Reels)

## Versioning

- v1: 2019 (founding)
- v2: 2022 (post-second-round refresh)
- v3: 2025-11
- v4 expected 2027

## How brand decisions get made

- Routine creative work: VP of Marketing approves
- Major campaigns or repositioning: COO + CEO sign-off
- Brand-relevant clinic-level decisions (e.g., naming a new flagship): RMD + COO + VP Marketing
