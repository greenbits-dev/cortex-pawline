# 2025 End-of-Year Review

The year-in-review document shared with all Pawline staff. Audience: everyone. Tone: warm, direct, no marketing gloss. Owner: [[dr-sarah-okafor]] (CEO writes the foreword) + Marcus Kim + Elena Vasquez (review-of-the-year sections).

## Foreword (Sarah)

> 2025 was the year we earned permission to slow down on purpose. We grew into 80 clinics; the work that grows with us is harder than we thought it would be. The integration debt that took us into the Q3 2026 pause decision had been quietly accumulating since mid-2024. Naming it, in late 2025, is one of the things I'm proudest of as a leadership team.

## The year by the numbers

- **Revenue**: $241M (target $235M, +3% over plan)
- **EBITDA margin**: 17.0% (target 16.5%, +50 bps)
- **Acquisitions closed**: 14 clinics (target 11-13)
- **DVM headcount**: 296 at year-end (vs 268 at start)
- **DVM retention (annual)**: 89.4% (KPI target ≥88%)
- **NPS (network average)**: 56 (KPI target ≥55)
- **Clinical Quality Review pass rate**: 92.3% (KPI target ≥90%)

## Big-stroke decisions

1. **Larkspur second round closed Q1** ([[2025-01-20-larkspur-second-round]])
2. **Surgical procurement centralized** ([[2025-04-15-centralize-surgical-procurement]] — $2.7M annualized savings)
3. **Telehealth pilot launched** ([[2025-08-04-launch-telehealth-pilot]] — 6 clinics, 240% YoY growth on small base)
4. **Medical Director Dyad rolled out** ([[2025-11-10-medical-director-dyad-rollout]])

## What worked

- **Cohort-vs-cohort improvement**: 2024 acquisition cohort outperformed 2023 ([[acquired-cohort-2024-outperforming-2023]])
- **Lab consolidation savings continuing** beyond the modeled estimates
- **NPS uplift in West region** as the dyad model matured
- **Surgical revenue growth**: surgical-active clinics showed 14% YoY growth, 2x the GP rate

## What didn't

- **Texas pipeline thinning** ([[texas-acquisition-pipeline-thinning]]) — TX growth came in below plan
- **West ROD vacancy** at year-end — search has been longer than anticipated
- **Charge-capture leakage** ([[charge-capture-leakage-4-to-6-percent]]) wasn't materially closed in 2025; v2 protocols and AI scribe are the 2026 levers
- **Dental coding miss** ([[dental-grade-3-undercoded-by-22-percent]]) — known and addressed in v2, but real

## What we're watching going into 2026

- The [[2026-01-22-q3-ma-pause]] holding without resentment building
- The PIMS migration backlog clearing on schedule
- The clinical-protocol v2 adoption curve
- The CAC trend ([[client-acquisition-cost-rising-q1-2026]]) that surfaced late in Q4
