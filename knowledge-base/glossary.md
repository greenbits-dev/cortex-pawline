# Glossary

Vet medicine, business, and Pawline-specific terms. Linked to atomic concepts where they exist.

## A

- **ADR (Average Doctor Revenue)** — production per DVM, generally measured per shift or per month. See [[average-doctor-revenue]].
- **Antech** — primary outside-lab vendor for Pawline. Consolidated from a 3-vendor mix in 2024. See `lab-vendor-consolidation-savings`.
- **Anesthesia tier** — Pawline's three-level classification (1/2/3) of anesthesia procedures, gating who can administer and what equipment is required. See [[anesthesia-tier-policy]].
- **Avimark** — legacy PIMS still in ~12 acquired clinics. Migrating to Cornerstone. See [[pims-migration-pathway]].

## C

- **Charge capture** — the process of ensuring everything done in a visit ends up on the invoice. Industry leakage runs 3-7%; Pawline's measured rate is 4-6%. See [[charge-capture]].
- **Clinic tier (A/B/C)** — Pawline's clinic classification by capability and revenue band. Tier A = flagship with surgical suites + specialty support; Tier B = full general practice; Tier C = limited-service satellite. See [[clinic-tier-system]].
- **Cluster** — Pawline term for a regional group of 8-15 clinics sharing an RMD/ROD pair, lab routing, and surgical suite access. See [[regional-cluster-economics]].
- **Cohort (acquisition cohort)** — clinics acquired in the same calendar year, tracked together for integration progress and margin recovery.
- **Controlled substances** — DEA Schedule II-V drugs handled in clinics. Tightly logged. See [[controlled-substances-handling]] and [[controlled-substances-policy]].
- **Cornerstone** — Idexx's Practice Information Management System. Pawline's standard PIMS as of 2024. See [[cornerstone-pims]].

## D

- **DEA registration** — every clinic that handles controlled substances has its own DEA registration. Required by 21 CFR 1301.
- **Dental grading** — Pawline's 1-4 grade scale for periodontal disease severity, branching the treatment plan. See [[dental-grading-system]].
- **DVM** — Doctor of Veterinary Medicine. The primary clinical role.

## E

- **EBITDA add-back** — adjustments made to reported EBITDA in QofE to normalize for non-recurring items, owner perks, etc. Pawline has a published list of accepted vs. rejected add-backs. See [[ebitda-add-backs-policy]].
- **eVetPractice** — legacy cloud-based PIMS, in ~5 acquired clinics. Migrating to Cornerstone.
- **Equity participation program** — Pawline's restricted stock program for tenured DVMs and operations leaders. See [[equity-participation-program]].

## F

- **Fivetran** — ETL tool moving data from Cornerstone, Salesforce, ADP, NetSuite into the Snowflake warehouse. See [[data-warehouse-architecture]].
- **Flagship clinic** — Tier A clinic anchoring a region, providing surgical suite + ER/specialty referral hub.

## G

- **GP (General Practice)** — wellness + sick-visit care. ~85% of Pawline visit volume.

## H

- **Hub-and-spoke** — Pawline's clinic operating model. A flagship anchors a region; satellites refer surgical/specialty work in. See [[hub-and-spoke-clinic-model]].
- **Hold period** — PE sponsor's expected time until exit. Larkspur's hold horizon for Pawline is ~5 years from second round (2023).

## I

- **IDEXX** — diagnostic equipment + Cornerstone PIMS vendor. Also Pawline's secondary lab vendor.
- **IDEXX Neo** — Idexx's cloud-based PIMS for smaller practices. ~3 Pawline clinics still use it.
- **Integration debt** — accumulated unfinished integration work across multiple acquired clinics. The reason for the [[2026-01-22-q3-ma-pause]].

## L

- **Larkspur Capital** — Pawline's PE sponsor. First investment 2019, second round 2023.

## M

- **Medical Director (MD)** — clinic-level senior DVM with quality oversight. Distinct from Regional Medical Director.
- **Medical director dyad** — Pawline's regional leadership structure pairing a Regional Medical Director (clinical) with a Regional Operations Director (non-clinical). See [[medical-director-dyad]].

## N

- **NetSuite** — Pawline's ERP for finance. Replaces clinic-level QuickBooks at integration.

## P

- **Pawline Endorsement** — co-branding model where an acquired clinic retains its legacy local name with "A Pawline Practice" added. ~40% of acquired clinics use this.
- **PIMS (Practice Information Management System)** — software that runs the clinic. Patient records, appointments, invoicing, inventory.
- **Production vs collection** — accrual revenue (production) vs. cash received (collection). See [[production-vs-collection]].
- **ProSal** — production-based DVM compensation: a base salary that floats up if a percentage of production exceeds it. Industry standard. See [[2026-04-25-comp-restructure-2027]] for Pawline's planned change.

## Q

- **QBR (Quarterly Business Review)** — Pawline's quarterly forum where regional VPs report to the exec team.
- **QofE (Quality of Earnings)** — the financial diligence Pawline runs on every acquisition target before LOI. See [[quality-of-earnings-process]].

## R

- **RMD (Regional Medical Director)** — DVM responsible for clinical quality across a region. Half of the dyad. See [[medical-director-dyad]].
- **ROD (Regional Operations Director)** — non-clinical operator paired with an RMD. The other half of the dyad.
- **Rollover equity** — when a seller takes part of the deal proceeds as equity in Pawline rather than cash. See [[seller-rollover-equity]].
- **RPV (Revenue Per Visit)** — average dollars billed per appointment. The headline GP productivity metric. See [[revenue-per-visit]].

## S

- **Salesforce** — Pawline's client outreach + reactivation tool. Not the PIMS.
- **Sigma** — Pawline's BI tool of choice (Sigma Computing, Snowflake-native). See [[clinical-kpi-definitions]].
- **Snowflake** — Pawline's data warehouse. See [[data-warehouse-architecture]].
- **Specialty** — non-GP services: cardiology, internal medicine, dermatology, oncology. ~5% of Pawline volume, concentrated at flagship clinics.
- **Standard of care (SOC)** — Pawline's clinical protocol library. See [[standard-of-care-protocols]] and [[clinical-protocols-policy]].

## T

- **Tier A/B/C** — see Clinic tier.
- **Telehealth** — virtual GP/triage visits. Pilot launched 2025-08, scaling cautiously. See [[2025-08-04-launch-telehealth-pilot]].
- **Triage acuity** — red/yellow/green classification at intake. See [[triage-acuity-levels]].

## U

- **Urgent Care** — same-day non-life-threatening visits. ~10% of Pawline volume. See [[urgent-care-vs-er-vs-gp]].

## V

- **Vest schedule** — equity grant vesting. Pawline's standard is 4-year cliff/quarterly. See [[equity-vesting-policy]].

## Specialized roles inside Pawline

- **CCO** — Chief Commercial Officer (role currently rolled into COO).
- **CMO** — Chief Medical Officer. Dr Elena Vasquez.
- **CPO** — Chief People Officer / Head of People & Culture. Lisa Rodriguez.
- **Head of M&A** — Dr James Okwu, DVM background, owns deal origination through close.

## Larkspur-specific

- **Hold period** — Larkspur's typical PE hold is 5-7 years. Pawline is ~3 years into the second-round hold.
- **Quarterly partner touch** — informal monthly check-in between Pawline CFO and Larkspur partner; formal quarterly board.
